## Frameworks

### Framework 1: BPM Method (Brand-driven Performance Marketing)

The BPM Method is Mandalia's master framework. It resolves the false dichotomy between brand marketing and performance marketing by proving that brand-first creative consistently outperforms pure direct-response creative at scale on Facebook/Meta.

[SOURCE: BPM Method -- Depesh Mandalia]

**Core Thesis:**
Brand and performance are not opposites. They are the same discipline executed at different time horizons. Brand creative builds trust, recognition, and emotional resonance. Performance creative converts that trust into measurable action. When you combine them -- when your performance ads ALSO build brand -- you create a compounding advantage that pure DR (direct response) cannot match.

**The BPM Spectrum:**

```
BRAND <─────────────────────────────────> PERFORMANCE
  |                                            |
  Trust                                    Conversion
  Awareness                                   ROAS
  Emotion                                    Action
  Long-term                                Short-term
  Story                                      Offer
```

**Key Insight:** The winning position is NOT at either extreme. It is in the overlap zone where brand and performance merge. An ad that tells your brand story AND drives a click. A video that builds trust AND includes a CTA. A campaign that grows your audience AND generates sales.

**BPM Method Application Process:**

```yaml
bpm_method:
  step_1_brand_audit:
    action: "Assess current brand assets, voice, positioning, and creative library"
    questions:
      - "What does the brand stand for beyond its product?"
      - "What emotional territory does the brand own?"
      - "What visual and tonal identity exists?"
      - "What brand assets can be leveraged in paid media?"
    output: "Brand Asset Inventory"

  step_2_performance_audit:
    action: "Analyse current ad account metrics, campaign structure, and creative performance"
    questions:
      - "What is current ROAS across campaigns?"
      - "Which creatives have the highest CTR, lowest CPA?"
      - "What is the creative refresh cadence?"
      - "How is the account structured (testing vs scaling)?"
    output: "Performance Baseline Report"

  step_3_bpm_creative_strategy:
    action: "Design creative that lives in the overlap zone -- brand + performance"
    principles:
      - "Every performance ad must reinforce brand identity"
      - "Every brand ad must include a measurable action"
      - "Creative should educate, entertain, OR inspire -- AND convert"
      - "The hook must stop the scroll AND communicate brand"
    output: "BPM Creative Brief"

  step_4_testing_execution:
    action: "Deploy BPM creatives through GT-CBO Testing Phase"
    method: "Use Creative Testing Matrix with BPM-scored variants"
    output: "Validated BPM Creatives (winners identified)"

  step_5_scale_with_brand:
    action: "Scale winning BPM creatives while maintaining brand consistency"
    rule: "As budget increases, brand elements must strengthen, not weaken"
    output: "Scaled BPM Campaigns"
```

**BPM Scoring (for creative evaluation):**

```
BPM Score = (Brand Signal x 0.4) + (Performance Signal x 0.6)

Brand Signal (1-10):
  - Brand recognition: Does the ad feel like YOUR brand? (colours, tone, visual identity)
  - Emotional resonance: Does it trigger the intended emotion?
  - Differentiation: Would a competitor be uncomfortable running this ad?
  - Memorability: Would someone remember the brand 24 hours later?

Performance Signal (1-10):
  - Hook strength: Does it stop the scroll in 3 seconds?
  - Value proposition clarity: Is the offer immediately understood?
  - CTA effectiveness: Is the desired action clear and compelling?
  - Urgency/scarcity: Is there a reason to act now?

Scoring:
  - BPM < 4: Reject. Either brand-dead or performance-dead.
  - BPM 4-6: Acceptable. Can run but needs iteration.
  - BPM 6-8: Strong. Prioritise for testing.
  - BPM > 8: Exceptional. Fast-track to scale.
```

### Framework 2: GT-CBO Method (3-Phase Campaign Lifecycle)

The GT-CBO Method is Mandalia's operational framework for managing campaigns through Facebook's CBO (Campaign Budget Optimization) system. It defines three distinct phases, each with its own rules, metrics, and decision criteria.

[SOURCE: GT-CBO Method -- Depesh Mandalia / SMC Media]

**Overview:**

```
Phase 1: CBO TESTING ──> Phase 2: CBO PROSPECTING ──> Phase 3: CBO SCALING
  (Find winners)           (Validate at scale)          (Scale profitably)
```

**Phase 1: CBO Testing**

Purpose: Discover the best ad/adset combinations using controlled CBO testing.

```yaml
cbo_testing:
  objective: "Find winning creative + audience combinations"
  campaign_structure:
    type: "CBO (Campaign Budget Optimization)"
    budget: "Daily budget = target CPA x 3-5 (per campaign)"
    adsets_per_campaign: "3-5 (varied audiences)"
    ads_per_adset: "3-5 (varied creatives)"
    total_combinations: "9-25 per campaign"

  audience_strategy:
    adset_1: "Broad targeting (age + gender + geo only)"
    adset_2: "Interest-based targeting (primary interest stack)"
    adset_3: "Lookalike 1% (based on best customers)"
    adset_4: "Lookalike 2-5% (based on best customers)"
    adset_5: "Stacked interests (secondary interest stack)"

  creative_strategy:
    ad_1: "Video (story-driven, BPM score > 6)"
    ad_2: "Image (product-focused, strong hook)"
    ad_3: "Carousel (multi-product or feature walkthrough)"
    ad_4: "UGC-style (testimonial or unboxing)"
    ad_5: "Brand-forward (emotional, BPM score > 7)"

  decision_criteria:
    minimum_data: "At least 1000 impressions per ad OR 3x target CPA spend"
    winner_definition: "CPA < target CPA AND CTR > benchmark AND frequency < 2"
    kill_criteria: "CPA > 2x target after minimum data threshold"
    learning_period: "Do NOT touch for 3-5 days (let CBO learn)"

  output: "Validated ad/adset combinations (winners) ready for Prospecting"

  rules:
    - "NEVER optimise during learning period (first 3-5 days)"
    - "NEVER judge a creative with less than 1000 impressions"
    - "NEVER run only 1 creative per adset (minimum 3)"
    - "Test ONE variable at a time when possible"
    - "Kill losing ads, not losing adsets (unless adset CPA is 3x target)"
```

**Phase 2: CBO Prospecting**

Purpose: Validate that winning combinations from Phase 1 work at increased budget.

```yaml
cbo_prospecting:
  objective: "Confirm winners scale before full investment"
  campaign_structure:
    type: "CBO"
    budget: "2-3x testing budget"
    adsets: "Only winning adsets from Phase 1"
    ads: "Only winning ads from Phase 1"

  validation_criteria:
    period: "7-14 days at increased budget"
    stable_cpa: "CPA within 20% of testing phase CPA"
    stable_roas: "ROAS within 20% of testing phase ROAS"
    frequency: "Below 2.5 (no fatigue signals)"
    ctr: "Maintaining or improving vs testing phase"

  decision_points:
    proceed_to_scale: "CPA stable, ROAS stable, frequency low for 7+ days"
    return_to_testing: "CPA > 30% above testing phase after 7 days"
    kill: "CPA > 50% above testing phase after 14 days"

  output: "Validated campaigns ready for Phase 3 scaling"

  rules:
    - "Increase budget by maximum 20-30% every 3 days"
    - "NEVER double budget overnight"
    - "Monitor frequency daily -- it is the early warning system"
    - "If CPA spikes, check frequency first, then creative fatigue"
    - "Maintain creative refresh pipeline (new creatives always in Phase 1)"
```

**Phase 3: CBO Scaling**

Purpose: Scale validated campaigns to maximum profitable volume.

```yaml
cbo_scaling:
  objective: "Maximise volume at or above target ROAS"
  campaign_structure:
    type: "CBO"
    budget: "Scaling incrementally (20-30% increases every 3-5 days)"
    adsets: "Consolidated winners"
    ads: "Top 2-3 performers"

  scaling_methods:
    vertical_scaling:
      description: "Increase budget on existing campaigns"
      rule: "Max 20-30% increase every 3-5 days"
      risk: "Medium (CBO needs time to recalibrate)"
    horizontal_scaling:
      description: "Duplicate winning campaigns with new audiences"
      rule: "Create new campaign with same winning ads, different audience"
      risk: "Low (avoids disrupting winning campaign)"
    creative_scaling:
      description: "Add new creatives to winning campaigns"
      rule: "Add 2-3 new creatives every 5-7 days to combat fatigue"
      risk: "Low-Medium (new creatives may underperform)"

  monitoring:
    daily: "CPA, ROAS, frequency, spend pacing"
    weekly: "Cost per unique, attribution, creative fatigue indicators"
    biweekly: "Account-level efficiency, audience overlap, saturation"

  kill_switches:
    frequency_cap: "IF frequency > 3 -> pause and refresh creative"
    cpa_spike: "IF CPA > 40% above target for 3 consecutive days -> reduce budget 20%"
    roas_floor: "IF ROAS < breakeven for 2 consecutive days -> pause and diagnose"
    fatigue_signal: "IF CTR drops > 30% from peak -> creative refresh needed"

  output: "Profitably scaled campaigns at maximum volume"

  rules:
    - "Scale SLOWLY. Patience is a competitive advantage."
    - "ALWAYS have creatives in Phase 1 testing pipeline"
    - "Scaling is not a destination -- it is a maintenance operation"
    - "When in doubt, reduce budget 20% rather than turn off"
    - "The creative pipeline is your insurance policy against fatigue"
```

```
  |
  +-- PROSPECTING CAMPAIGNS (Phase 2 -- GT-CBO Prospecting)
  |     |
  |     +-- Campaign: PROSP_[Product]_[Date]
  |           +-- Adset: [Winner from Testing]
  |           +-- Adset: [Winner from Testing]
  |
  +-- SCALING CAMPAIGNS (Phase 3 -- GT-CBO Scaling)
  |     |
  |     +-- Campaign: SCALE_[Product]_[Date]
  |           +-- Adset: [Validated from Prospecting]
  |           +-- Ads: Top 2-3 performers
  |
  +-- RETARGETING CAMPAIGNS (Always On)
  |     |
  |     +-- Campaign: RT_[Funnel Stage]
  |           +-- Adset: Website Visitors 7d (excl. purchasers)
```

### Framework 4: Creative Testing Matrix

Mandalia's systematic approach to testing creative variables independently, ensuring statistically meaningful results.

[SOURCE: Depesh Mandalia / BPM Method]

**Principle:** Never test multiple variables simultaneously. Isolate ONE variable per test to know EXACTLY what caused the result.

**The 4 Core Variables:**

```yaml
creative_testing_matrix:
  variable_1_hook:
    description: "First 3 seconds of video / first line of copy / main visual element"
    why_test: "The hook determines whether anyone sees the rest of your ad"
    test_method: "Same body + CTA, different hooks"
    variants_minimum: 3
    examples:
      - "Question hook: 'Are you still wasting money on ads that don't convert?'"
      - "Statement hook: 'We spent $2M on Facebook Ads so you don't have to.'"
      - "Contrarian hook: 'Stop optimising your ads. Start fixing your creative.'"
      - "Social proof hook: '14,000 businesses use this exact method.'"
      - "Pain hook: 'Your ads are failing because of ONE mistake.'"

  variable_2_visual:
    description: "The visual format and style of the ad"
    why_test: "Visual format affects thumb-stop rate and engagement pattern"
    test_method: "Same hook + copy + CTA, different visual formats"
    variants_minimum: 3
    examples:
      - "Talking head video (founder/expert)"
      - "Product demo / unboxing"
      - "UGC testimonial"
      - "Motion graphics / animated"
      - "Static image with bold text overlay"
      - "Carousel with sequential story"

  variable_3_copy:
    description: "The body text / script / narrative of the ad"
    why_test: "Copy determines the depth of engagement and qualification"
    test_method: "Same hook + visual + CTA, different body copy"
    variants_minimum: 3
    examples:
      - "Long-form story (problem-agitation-solution)"
      - "Short-form direct (benefit + CTA in 2 lines)"
      - "Listicle (5 reasons why...)"
      - "Testimonial-led (customer story)"
      - "Data-led (statistics + proof)"

  variable_4_cta:
    description: "The call to action -- what you ask the viewer to do"
    why_test: "CTA determines the conversion action and landing experience"
    test_method: "Same hook + visual + copy, different CTAs"
    variants_minimum: 2
    examples:
      - "'Shop Now' (direct purchase intent)"
      - "'Learn More' (lower friction, more info seekers)"
      - "'Get Your Free Guide' (lead generation)"
      - "'Watch the Full Video' (engagement depth)"
      - "'Book a Call' (high-ticket qualification)"
```

**Testing Sequence (Priority Order):**

```
1. HOOK first (highest impact -- determines if ad is seen at all)
2. VISUAL second (determines engagement format)
3. COPY third (determines qualification and persuasion depth)
4. CTA fourth (determines conversion path)
```

**Testing Rules:**

```yaml
testing_rules:
  rule_1: "ONE variable per test. Always."
  rule_2: "Minimum 3 variants per variable"
  rule_3: "Minimum 1000 impressions per variant before judging"
  rule_4: "Statistical significance > gut feeling"
  rule_5: "Document EVERY test result (win, lose, or draw)"
  rule_6: "Winning hooks get paired with winning visuals in next round"
  rule_7: "Losing variants are data, not failures"
  rule_8: "Refresh the matrix every 2-4 weeks to combat creative fatigue"
  rule_9: "The best-performing creative today is not the best-performing creative next month"
  rule_10: "Never stop testing. Ever. The testing pipeline IS the business."
```

## Decision Heuristics

Each heuristic includes WHEN to apply it and the specific threshold that triggers action.

```yaml
heuristics:

  h1_cpa_diagnosis:
    rule: "IF CPA > 2x target after 1000 impressions -> kill the ad"
    when: "During Phase 1 CBO Testing, after minimum data threshold"
    rationale: "An ad that costs 2x target after sufficient data will not improve. Cut losses and test the next hypothesis."
    action: "Pause the ad. Log the result. Deploy next creative variant."

  h2_frequency_warning:
    rule: "IF frequency > 2.5 -> prepare creative refresh. IF frequency > 3 -> execute refresh immediately."
    when: "During Phase 2 Prospecting or Phase 3 Scaling, monitored daily"
    rationale: "Frequency > 2.5 means the same people are seeing the ad repeatedly. Creative fatigue is imminent."
    action: "Phase 1: new creatives into testing. Phase 2/3: rotate winning creatives or add new ones."

  h3_scaling_pace:
    rule: "IF scaling -> increase budget by max 20-30% every 3-5 days"
    when: "During Phase 3 CBO Scaling"
    rationale: "CBO needs time to recalibrate delivery after budget changes. Doubling overnight destroys optimisation."
    action: "Set calendar reminders for budget increases. Document each increase with before/after metrics."

  h4_roas_floor:
    rule: "IF ROAS < breakeven for 2 consecutive days -> pause and diagnose"
    when: "During any active campaign"
    rationale: "Losing money for 2 days is acceptable as a blip. Losing for 2 consecutive days is a trend."
    action: "Check: creative fatigue? audience saturation? competitor activity? landing page issues?"

  h5_learning_phase_respect:
    rule: "IF campaign is in learning phase -> DO NOT make changes for 3-5 days"
    when: "After launching any new campaign, adset, or ad"
    rationale: "Facebook's algorithm needs ~50 conversion events to exit learning phase. Changes reset the clock."
    action: "Wait. Monitor but do not touch. Record data daily."

  h6_creative_pipeline:
    rule: "IF no creatives in Phase 1 testing -> HALT scaling and prioritise new creative production"
    when: "Always, monitored weekly"
    rationale: "Without a testing pipeline, you are one creative fatigue event away from account collapse."
    action: "Deploy Creative Testing Matrix. Brief 3-5 new creatives. Launch into Phase 1."

  h7_broad_vs_interest:
    rule: "IF spending > $1000/day and broad targeting CPA < interest targeting CPA -> shift budget to broad"
    when: "During Phase 2 or Phase 3, with sufficient data"
    rationale: "At scale, Facebook's algorithm with broad targeting often outperforms manual interest targeting."
    action: "Gradually shift budget. Never go 100% broad overnight. Monitor for 7 days."

  h8_budget_allocation:
    rule: "IF total budget -> allocate 60% scaling, 20% prospecting, 20% testing"
    when: "When designing budget allocation for an established account"
    rationale: "Testing feeds the pipeline. Prospecting validates. Scaling generates revenue."
```

## Anti-Patterns

### NEVER Do

- **Scale without validating** -- Never skip Phase 2 Prospecting. A winner in testing at $100/day may collapse at $500/day.
- **Double budgets overnight** -- CBO needs time to recalibrate. Maximum 20-30% increase every 3-5 days.
- **Run 1 creative per adset** -- Minimum 3. Without variance, there is no learning.
- **Edit during learning phase** -- Wait 3-5 days. Changes reset the algorithm's optimisation clock.
- **Ignore frequency** -- Frequency is the early warning system. Above 2.5 = prepare. Above 3 = act immediately.
- **Blame the platform** -- "Facebook is broken" is almost always code for "my creative is stale." Fix the creative first.
- **Run zero testing budget** -- Even at $50,000/day spend, at least 15-20% goes to Phase 1 testing. The pipeline is the product.
- **Rely on a single winning creative** -- One creative is one point of failure. Always have replacements in the pipeline.
- **Copy competitor ads verbatim** -- Study them for patterns. Never copy them. Your brand is your competitive advantage.
- **Set it and forget it** -- Media buying is active management. Daily monitoring, weekly analysis, biweekly restructuring.
- **Use vague metrics** -- "It's doing okay" is not a metric. CPA is $X. ROAS is Y. Frequency is Z. Numbers or nothing.
- **Boost posts as a strategy** -- Boosting is not media buying. It is a button designed for people who do not know how to use Ads Manager.

### Audit Rule
- **ALWAYS analyse ONLY active campaigns, adsets, and ads** (blue toggle ON). Deactivated elements do not require analysis. They are historical data, not current performance. This applies at ALL levels: campaign > adset > ad.
